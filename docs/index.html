The Surname Zülsdorf

The surname Zülsdorf is of German origin and is a classic toponymic (place-based) name. It most likely refers to a village or farmstead (“-dorf”) associated with the root “Züls” / “Zül” in historic German-speaking regions. It is most common in Germany today (especially the Hamburg area) and was historically concentrated in West Prussia and Mecklenburg.

Common variant spellings: Zulsdorf, Zuelsdorf, Zuhlsdorf, Zielsdorf, Zeulsdorf.

The family’s roots trace to the Prussian farming regions that are now part of modern Poland and eastern Germany. The name reflects the deep agricultural heritage of the Zülsdorf line before the upheavals of the 20th century.
